issue 01: interview

An in-depth conversation with Leonid Kotel about language, memory, and digital transformation.

What inspired your interest in language transformation?

You could say it started with my early experiments with digital text. The way language breaks down and rebuilds itself in different contexts fascinates me.

How do you see the relationship between spoken and written language?

They're worlds apart yet intimately connected. When text is transcribed from speech, it loses so many dimensions - tone, pauses, gestures. But it also gains permanence and new interpretive possibilities.

Your work often deals with corruption of meaning. Can you elaborate?

Corruption isn't always negative. When language breaks down or transforms, new meanings emerge. Think of a glitch as a creative force rather than an error.
